Overview

The PMT function is one of many financial functions in Excel. It is used in computing regular remittance or installment for a loan. Given the loan value, repayment intervals, and the interest rate, the NPER function can be used to evaluate payment for the loan.

Aim

To obtain the loan repayment interval

Return Amount

The loan value in number format

The Formula

=PMT (rate, nper, pv, [fv], [type])

Description

• rate- The scheduled interest rate for the loan

• nper- The aggregate value to be paid for the loan.

• pv – The gross value of the loan payment today called the present value

• fv - [elective] The amount expected at the end of the last payment known as the future value. Set to default (0)

• type- (elective) upon meeting expected payment. 0= for end period, 1= for beginning period

When to use

The PMT function is used to quantify future remittance for a loan. Supposing you have a fixed payment and interest rate plan, the PMT function can be used to figure out the future payments for a loan

Example

Mr. Ali borrowed $20,000 from a bank to be repaid after 24 months with a yearly interest rate of 10%, PMT can evaluate the installment and interest to be paid on monthly bases.

Hints:

• Remittance or payment executed by PMT comprises principal and interest only. Other fees including taxes, deferred, withheld or retain payments are not included.

• Do not vary the designated units for rate and nper. To calculate monthly payments on a five-year loan at a yearly rate of 15%, use 15%/12 for the rate and 5*12 for nper. To estimate yearly payment on the same loan, use 15% for rate and 5 for nper
