1. The Marquee

image

A Marquee is a big kind of tent often used as temporary housing. A Marquee can be put into numerous uses including fairs, large weddings, circus, banquets, worship centers, banquets, revival, and corporate entertainment programs. Typically, the Marquee is finished with canvas. New and recent tents are likely to be made of Polythene or PVC for increased quality. You can get the Marquee up to about 20 feet (6.1 m) wide. Some Marquee especially those that measure up to 30 to 150 feet (9.1 to 45.7 meters) widths are costly and can be very technical; requiring specific tools and skilled personnel to put up. Numerous companies have relatively great size; (9 m to 45 m wide) marquee. However, these tents are erected and maintained by trained experts.

2. The Canopy tent

image

The canopy tents area is suitable for public functions including community day ceremonies, fairs, and carnivals. In most cases, these tents do not need to be aesthetically alluring. The main essence of the canopy tent is to shield you from rain or sunlight. Canopy tents do not arrive with a high peak and owing to the size, the style of the roof could be flattened out. Oftentimes, canopies are lightweight and by their make are not furnished with sidewalls and cannot withstand heavy wind and rain. Canopies that are smaller in size are designed for smaller or backyard functions. For those seeking quality at an affordable cost, the canopy tent is economical. Most times, they are simple to erect and can be put up even by the client.

3. The Traditional Pole Tent

image

For good reasons, the traditional pole tent has been constantly put to use by many individuals. The traditional pole tent fits almost all types of events. They are finished with high peaks and arrive in varied width, and sizes from 20 to 80 inches. The traditional pole tents are fancied for corporate events, wedding ceremonies, etc. Most times, they can be made beautiful and elegantly decorated by using pole skirts and fabric and at other times, used as they arrive; mostly for casual functions. One setback of this pole is that large footprints are needed to enhance staking. Staking is essential for installation, however, the center pole and those inside the tents obstruct views. The traditional pole tent also has sidewalls and arrive in various designs. If required, the clients choose from the options available for installation.

image

What to Consider When Choosing a Tent

Before choosing any of the above tents, here are some factors to be considered.

• The Cost: Unlike the less costly tents that are rather heavy, not durable and cannot withstand water tension, the expensive counterparts are lightweight has high water stress capacity and are durable

• The need for the tent: To use any tent, consider the size. How simple is it to pitch the tent? What will be the duration of usage? A day, week, or months?

• Be conscious of the Season: Tents that may be ideal to use during the winter may not be ideal during the summer. Most tents come with labels and inscriptions by the manufacturers. A tent that is labeled: “One-season” meets only the needs of the summer and can withstand light showers while a tent that is labeled three-season can meet the demand of the autumn, summer and spring. This means that it is capable of weathering fairly heavy rains, and light snow. Others are labeled 4 seasons: This is ideal for winter and all extremes weather

• Size of tent: What are the ages of your invitees? How many invitees are you expecting? Answers to these questions will help in deciding the size of the tent and the features to seek for in the tent.

• Tent color: For some functions or events, there is the need to tone down the visual influence of the tents. Some functions require colors with low visibility while others need highly visible or colorful pigments. More so, bright colored suits tents which the activities going on has an attendant high-risk implication. For instance; in case of emergencies, bright-colored tents are easy to spot. They are also essential in locations where oncoming vehicles may not see quickly low visibility tent. This may prevent it from running over the venue.

• Installation time and effort: Certain kinds of tents demand less effort to set up. Others need a great deal of effort to do the same. As a point to note: a highly robust tent will require more effort and time to install and dismantle as well. Some designs, style, and finishing are easy to set up hence demand less time and effort while others are not

• Weather conditions: A tent can either withstand cold weather or rainy situations. As earlier mentioned, it can be two, three, or a four-season tent. While the two-season tent suits the hot/warm temperature but fails to stand windy and rainy weather. Three-season tents withstand rain and wind but not heavy snow. The Four-season on the other hand can stand any weather situation. Note that the design, style, and other tent attributes may change depending on the seasons it can endure or cope with.
