A Tiebet Travel

Regarded as the roof of the world; and dazzled by the sunset of the colossal Mount Everest, Tibet; a unique cultural beauty is situated North of the Himalayas. As a natural beauty, it is home to many visitors. Despite being branded by many as a difficult to access destination, it is entirely easy in actuality. Below are a few hints to guide you as you prepare for a Tibet travel.

  1. Autonomous Voyage to Tibet? It is a No

    As stipulated by the dictates of the Tibet Tourism Bureau, foreigners are not permitted to make autonomous voyage into Tibet. Officially recognized travel agency is scheduled in China to offer travel services and trip guide. You may also get your lodging needs fixed by the travel agency as well. While in Tibet, your tour guide will be handy to escort you through site attractions. Alternatively, you can connect with other organized tourist or demand a private tour guide from your agent. At the end of the day's engagements, by the leave of your agent, you are at liberty to move around municipalities like Lhasa, Gyantse, Shigatse, and Nyingchi.

  2. For Entrance into Tibet, an Entry Permit is Essential

    In addition to possessing an authentic international passport, and a Chinese visa, a Tibet entry permit is also needed from international visitors. Foreigners cannot board a train or flight to Tibet not until they acquire a Tibet travel permit often issued by the Tourism Bureau. To obtain the permit, which takes about 15 to 20 days, notify a travel agency in China to process an application for you.

  3. Journalists, diplomats are not licensed to Travel to Tibet with a Travel Agency

    It is correct that your Tibet trip has to be scheduled by a local travel agency but with exception to Journalists and Diplomats. Travel agency does not assist diplomats, journalist or employees from the media. These sets of person seek permission and approval to enter Tibet from the Chinese Foreign Affairs Office. Seemingly, it is irrelevant to apply for a China visa and Tibet permit as a diplomat or journalist from an agent.

  4. Check out Entry Options and Means into Tibet

    Direct flight into Tibet does not exist for international visitors with exceptions to Nepal which borders with Tibet and China. Irrespective of your nationality, a transit by flight to Nepal demands arriving first at Nepal or mainland China.
    • Board a direct flight from Nepal to Tibet through Kathmandu to Lhasa Gonggar International Airport
    • From Mainland China, direct flights exist from Beijing, Xi'an, Chongqing, Chengdu, and Kunming into Tibet. Alternatively, you can fly in from Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Hangzhou
    • Consider the railway as an alternative - Enter Tibet from mainland China cities by rail through Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Xi'an, Chengdu, Chongqing, Lanzhou, and Xining
    • Between Tibet and Nepal is a route which was destroyed by an earthquake in 2015. At the moment, re-construction is completed thus qualifying the road as an option for entry into Tibet. A transit from Lhasa to Kathmandu by road is now possible.

  5. There is no age Limit, but be Conscious

    Age limitation does not exist for visitors and foreigners coming into Tibet. However, here are some hints for you. For those planning a visit to Tibet with the aged and babies, there are no basic facilities to cater for their health needs in remote locations. It will be a bit tedious getting them to facilities in Lhasa if the need arises. Further, owing to high altitude, high blood pressure and heart disease patients are often advised to reconsider visiting Tibet.

  6. The Annual Tibet Closure

    In order to commemorate recent Tibetan historical events including 10th and 30th March, Lhasa and all other Tibet Autonomous Regions (TAR) are often closed a during this dates. This government imposed closure commences in early February to March end annually. International visitors are not expected to travel to Tibet Autonomous Region during this period. Travel Permits processing re-start every first Monday in March.

  7. Ideal Travel Periods

    An understanding of when to visit Tibet is crucial. A Tibet travel is best-done during early April through mid-June and also mid-September through November end. During this period, the temperature is relatively mild so that you can be sure of dry, bright and good mountain sights. More so, December through Mid February could also be appropriate for a Tibet travel

  8. Movements within Tibet

    Foreigners are not authorized to use general transportation while inTibet Municipalities. While in Lhasa, public transit can be used, but the movement to other areas of Tibet are done on a personal basis. On private provision, your travel agent would have to schedule a professional driver to move you around town. Most times, these agents arrange minivans and minibusses for organized visitors (small and large group respectively)

  9. Some Locations and Attractions are Restricted

    Not all places and attractions are opened for visit to international visitors in Tibet. Your trip agency will provide you with an overview of places to visit while in Tibet but for a start, check out the following locations with their requirements for entry.
  • For Lhasa, Shigatse, and Lake Namtso visit, Tibet Entry Permit is mandatory. So, apply for it prior to your departure.

  • To visit Nyingchi and Mount Everest, you must have acquired you Aliens' Travel Permit. On arrival at Tibet, apply for it.

  • For Bome, Rawu, Shannan, Naidong, Gajah, Nang County, and Ngari visit, you are expected to have a Military Area Entry Permit.

  • Permits are not given for entry into places including Tibetan Kings' Tombs and Lhamo Latso in Shannan, Brahmaputra Grand Canyon and Nayi Valley in Nyingchi, Yatung, Tsonag, and Chamdo.

  • A climbing permit is necessary to climb the Mount Everest. Apply for a climbing permit through a professional mountaineering firm or the General Administration of Sports of China.
