Emerging Trend in Human Resources (HR) Analytics

10 years ago, touting the importance of HR analytics probably had been an irrelevant idea because there was no possible value to be obtained from HR data. In recent time, the focus has been shifted to revolutionizing the potentials of HR with special considerations on core operations such as staff head counts, evaluation and appraisal, year’s average performance. However, today, trends in HR analytics offers wider capabilities to manage talent and make evidence-based people decisions.

What is Human Resource (HR) Analytics? It is the combination of statistical and analytical data as well as the relevant information to provide insight and aid decision-making to people management in the organization. HR analytics has witness a growing trend among businesses concerned with high value maximization. Today, there are evidences on new approach to data gathering, processing, estimation, reporting and usage. Evidential trends in HR analytics include;

Automated recruiting; HR analytics makes the identification of persons who best fits the organization's culture possible. Predictively, it aids in the identification of employee profiles that are both person and job fit for the company. Engagements, important contributions in terms of teamwork, attrition rates, and growth are some organizational metrics which HR analytics uses to establish the person fit.

Upskilling vs Hiring: HR analytics use an algorithm to predict what skills and other resources are required, but the organization does not have access to those core operational resources. HR analytics use an algorithm to predict what skills are required. HR analytics use data based on employee performance and background education to identify which employees can be upskilled to become specialists. Furthermore, it assists the business in filling talent gaps rather than investing a significant amount of time recruiting new hires and training them until they fit into the organization, hence cutting recruitment costs.

Development and Learning: Training executives now utilize data to identify the domain to center training needs. Executives are able to spot present and future skill gaps and present leaders with an evidences necessary to either advance or change training goals. With Analytics, HR can best determine the training tools and learning technique that would impact the most on staffs. In some firm, virtual reality now gives the interactive feel for both new and current hires with incisive feedback immediately. This aids communication from wherever the trainee and trainers are located.

Shared Services: The use of analytics in shared services to forecast future business operations, streamline strategies and spots potentials to reduce costs in all the entire spectrum has received support from all functions of the business. Using data, shared services managers strategically serve firms huge cost and build formidable end-to-end operational strategy.

Leadership: The position and duties of senior level executives is critical to organizational success but how can a firm ensure that appropriate leaders are in the right place? Data. The data so analyzed can predict and identify ideal people fit for leadership responsibilities and the staffs that would likely remained in the firm. When the right leaders are appropriately spotted, employees engagement and even Return on investment is guaranteed.

Other emerging trends in HR analytics include; HR analytics reduces employee turnover, uses chatbot to shorten the response time between callers who would naturally wait a long time to get HR respond to questions, personalize staff experience, automate activities which may not require humans, improve staff engagements and improve performance Measurement capacity of H, absence of face-time, work from home leading to work from anywhere, focus on cybersecurity.

Application of HR Analytics in Organizations

To many organizations, this data-driven concept remains quite new while others have already adopted it to their benefits and reaping the full potential. Below are some of HR analytics adopters.

1. Google: opines that people analytics enhances inclusive and decisive people management decisions. Peoples Operations the tagline for Google’s HR team apply HR analytics foster productivity, regulates, and survey the workforce. The feedback is used to optimize operations and culture. Consequently, Google has been able to obtain an average participation rate of 90%. This shows business prowess and morale.

2. Juniper Networks: Juniper Networks, a networking and cybersecurity solutions company anchors its performance on continuous and dynamic HR industry. They dare the consequences of risk of changing old processes for new once. Juniper Networks move a step further by adopting big data to validate performance and operations. Juniper applies adopts contemporary strategies to attract and retain present and future talent.

3. Wal-Mart: An American retail store analytics team saddled with responsibility of advancing insights to create value. Their main focus has been on employee turnover and workforce performance metrics. Adopt people analytics to identify roles and career progression for their staffs, with special focus on talent building. The essence is to surge performance, efficiency, and higher success.

4. Microsoft: For years now, Microsoft has evolved in HR analytics. One application of HR analytics is in statistics. It adopts statistical application to analyze employees profile and those who are likely to exit the firm. This assist them plan horizon of interventions including mentorship, growth plan and open communication opportunity. With focus on reducing the rate of turnover, Microsoft now reduce attrition rates.

Looking Ahead; HR Analytics Over the next 3 years

1. 2021 is almost over, the next phase for Human Resource is to forecast the dynamics beyond 2021. For 2022, firms should acknowledge the diverse -generational workplace; automation in work will bring in young employees who prioritize work-life balance and flexibility in working hour. Secondly, adapt to the gig economy: Surging contractors and freelancing. HR experts must acknowledge shift from permanent employment to maintaining agile operations and efficiency. Third, focus on data-driven HR: Adopt HR analytics to ensure high insights and make smarter operational leaders, predictions and decisions. Finally, HR chatbots for process: Deloitte opines that 22% of high performing HR firms will effect chatbots processes to automate high time consuming tasks such as answering FAQs on operations.

2. For 2023, Personalized Review System: The HR will institute super-personalized performance review. This would identify and acknowledge employee’s uniqueness. Next is the Future-forward HR leadership; Design of HR ecosystems and startups with technology at the focus of operation to appraise revenue-per-worker.

3. In 2024 and beyond, Agility will be considered; Agility implies that HR tasks be broken down into parts. Agile employees will have enormous work freedom and control. Surging Innovation: By 2024, innovation is paramount at all levels of work and roles. Micro-innovation concerns product-centred HR teams and the establishment of policies that impacts digitalization and Resource Sharing: The gig economy is one crafted around sharing human, physical and technological formations. Sharing resources will enable a competitive advantage especially in attracting future talent.

Negatives vs Positive impacts of HR Analytics Organizations

The First concern on HR analytics is the technology choice dilemma. Identifying the right data technologies compatible with in-house operations can be exhausting. Human resources professionals depend on IT-departments for those crucial technological choices; but no other department know exactly the needs of HR well enough as the human resources departments. HR analytics arrives with solutions to which the job of getting HR professionals educated on them can be very overwhelming and demanding. More so, HR managers aren’t data analyst by training. The lack the knowledge of data storage, management, data quality, data manipulation and visualization. Many even sweat at the mention of big-data, hence they may find it challenging. Finally, data is complex. Putting up a team to analyze firm data may be challenging, demanding and costly.

However, despite these concerns, there are notable positives. Heuvel and Bondarouk (2017) examined how HR analytics will effect corporate organization. About 70% of the respondents opined that the trend had a good impact, while 30% thought otherwise. Based on the responses of the survey participants, it was determined that the future of HR analytics will most likely be driven by a focus on integration. While the current focus of HR analytics is mostly on HR difficulties, which necessitates the use of HR data, the future emphasis is expected to shift to broader organizational challenges, which will necessitate data beyond the HR domain and even the business as a whole. Employee data will be integrated with data from finance, sales, marketing, social media, and personal devices. Regardless of whether this integration entails the development of enterprise-wide systems or data warehouses, all disciplines' data would be integrated in a single database for combined analysis hence; enhancing productivity, performance, more incisive decision, timely information retrieval. What Actions Should Firm Take?

1. Think Structure: Move from manual. Consider tools that are efficient and helpful and automated.

2. Adapt to Apps and Platforms; The platform and app ecosystem is exploding. Each action has an app. Invest in an HR digital software for adequate work human integration

3. Agility for value: Enable an HR processes that is more agile and reduce waste. Concentrate on value-added task by supporting departments and employees

4. Design a value friendly ecosystem: Value can be surged by the ecosystem where codes, data and competencies are shared among companies. Mutually dependent relationship is the key.

5. HR purpose, culture and values: Firms with purpose statements have greater likelihood of generating value that can forge a strong performance, employee confident, and customer trust. HR should identify and role model the desired mindsets and people associated with purpose and translating the purpose to employee norms and behaviors. Mores, institute purpose driven metrics

6. Consider Talents: Firms that priotizes talents are more certain to do three times better than their counterparts. A shift from the traditional HR process Contemporary system is ideal. HR needs to manage talents by developing capacity in analytics to mine data, hire, and train

7. Employee Experience through Learning: Better staff experience connotes better bottom-line. Firms to enable employee experience by training and coaching HR professionals on use of technology effectively.

8. Leadership building: Culture of leadership is the framework for which incredible financial performance is built. Leadership should be business focused. HR should therefore identify the roles of talents appropriately to create value.

9. Take Sound decisions: Decisions must be done and at the right time. HR analytics assist in empowers staffs to be risk takers especially when the culture rewards such persons.

HR analytics is a relatively new field in HR. It is impossible for corporations to function without data. In the digital age, there is an increase in HR experts who are competitive. Employee motivation, recruitment, and training are always in demand. Technology assists HR professionals in adapting to changing workforces and enables them to function at a higher level.
