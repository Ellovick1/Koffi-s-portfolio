The Deferred Action for Childhood (DACA) Framework

In what is considered many as patriotic sentiment the slogan "Put America First,” a President Trump’s immigration campaign aims at securing America’s institution, workers and alliances. It moved to back-pedal decades of US immigration policy which sought to accommodate what he termed the tired, weak and huddled masses from other countries aiming at breathing free using DACA. The DACA initiative; was an immigration policy inaugurated by the Obama regime in June 2012 to shield people residing in the United States without legal assent from repatriation and permitting them to attend school and work. Seemingly, as an administrative decision, the DACA policy reckons on deferred action, meaning the use of prosecutorial circumspection to grant school and work permit to the individual(s) from other countries for a definite duration as contained in the U.S citizenship and immigration services.

Initially, DACA only applied to individuals who satisfied the requirements scheduled below-

  • Must not be above the age of 31 as of June 15, 2012;
  • Arrived the United States before attaining the age of 16
  • Have lived in the United States of America right from June 15, 2007, up to the present moment.
  • Had been around in the United States on June 15, 2012, and during the period the request for examination of deferred action with USCIS was made.
  • Was without lawful status on June 15, 2012
  • Are at the moment schooling, received a certificate of high school completion from high school, have received a general education development (GED), or are an honorably discharged veteran of the Coast Guard or Armed Forces of the United States; and
  • Is devoid of conviction for a felony committed, notable criminal act, other wrongdoings, and not considered a threat to the security of the nation or the safety of the public.
The Move for DACA Expansion

On November 20, 2014, President Barak Obama advocated for the expansion of the DACA initiative to incorporate non-US citizens who had penetrated the United States before the year 2010, in place of the initial condition of having arrived before 2007. As reported by the USCIS, on June 2017, about 793,000 people had obtained the DACA relief since its initiation in 2012. As a product of an executive decision rather than legislative ratification or parliament, a discourse soon insured on whether the pronouncement on DACA was lawful under the constitution of the United States of America. Considered as an infraction of the Administrative Act, about 26 states questioned DACA expansion and moved for legal action with Texas pioneering the course.

On November 9, 2015, a prior injunction from a district court against the expansion of the initiative which was adjudged in support of the States was confirmed by the 5th US Circuit Court of Appeals. The injunction brought to halt the execution of the DACA expansion pending the adjudication of the policy. On June 23, 2016, a per curiam ruling emerged from the Supreme Court of the United States on DACA asserting the judgment of the 5th Circuit Court of Appeal. A per curiam opinion is ruling which does not name or specify a distinctive justice as the originator of the judgment. Consequent on an evenly divided interest of the court on the subject, the injunction was allowed in place.

The DACA Position

By implication, the concern of DACA pertains to non-US citizens who arrived the States as children without legal authorization. The policy which was instituted in 2012 was initially abrogated and nullified in September 2017 by the Acting Secretary of Homeland Security, Elaine Duke. The US Congress was not able to reach a legislative compromise to guarantee future DACA prospective which the government under the watch of Triumph had scheduled March 5, 2018, as it’s end date. Following the directive restraining the termination of DACA from Federal judges in San Francisco and New York, it then became officially compulsory for Triumph's government to resume the confirmation of DACA license.

In April 2018, adjudication by a US district judge mandated the Triumph's government to recommence the collection of fresh applications from non- US citizens soliciting interest through DACA. Initial rulings only demanded that the government extend the period of validity of a license. Following the failure of the Triumph's government to instantly carry out the order, a 90-day ultimatum was scheduled for the administration to make an explanation regarding its resolution to terminate DACA. On August 4, 2018, a ruling by a Federal judge mandated the Triumph’s government to reinstate DACA completely. This verdict was ratified by the 9th US Circuit Court of Appeals on November 8, 2018.
