Cannabis Investors Are Getting Value for their Money


Between January and March 2019, trends in marijuana stocks indicate a favorable return on investment. Since the onset of the year, the wide-ranging marijuana exchange-traded fund ETFMG Alternative Harvest ( NYSEMKT: MJ) has surged to 45% and consolidating more than $1.25 billion worth an asset. More so, in the period under review, shares of several firms have escalated over 50% in about 36 stocks owned by the Alternative Harvest ETF.

Seemingly, the Green Organic Dutchman (NASDAQOTH:TGODF ), HEXO (NYSEMKT:HEXO), and OrganiGram Holdings (NASDAQOTH:OGRMF amidst the ETF’s portfolio have been quite impressive, improving at a rate faster than the industry leads and a gross earning that is twice the size of what the marijuana ETF has offered its investors

An escalating Dutchman

Unlike the New York Stock Exchange or the Nasdaq Stock Market, shares of Green Organic Dutchman do not trade on the US stock exchange; hence low commercial activities for the US marijuana investors. Regardless of this fact, the Green Organic Dutchman has recorded a commanding performance in the Canadian market. On account of this, the company is considering an unlikely approach; one that will be supportive of devoted customers for a relatively long period.

Majority of firms in the industry have undertaken expansion programs aimed at meeting the increasing demand by intensifying supply. In the short-run, insufficient supply makes the schedule seemingly favored. Green Organic Dutchman tags this market reaction as short-sighted. Contrary to the views making the rounds, the company intends capturing the upside marijuana market by vending its organic product collection at a rate it considered a fair charge for customers willing to purchase. Apparently, this does not imply rigidity in the part of Green Organic Dutchman as plans are currently underway for expansion. Clearly, with this signal to the investors, Green Organic Dutchman is set to capture majority of the shares and to lead the market; offering quality cannabis and cannabis-related products.

HEXO re-enters the market

Not long ago, HEXO made a comeback to the NYSE and is attracting US investors in cannabis-related firms. In region within Canada, the Quebec-based firm is ideal and preferred. As part of its growth strategy, HEXO has acquired peer Newstrike Brands and also collaborates with Molson Coors to survey cannabis-infused drinks. It also plans to explore the European market; a region considered as legal. Having its processing plant in Greece, HEXO is set to supply marijuana-related products to the European Union territories. By extension, the ongoing market expansion is scheduled to complement returns from other HEXO projects including Hydropothecary ancillary and its sales of sublingual THC spray

OrganiGram makes remarkable flashes

Aside from focusing on the sales potentials for marijuana stocks, a few strategies have been cost centered. OrganiGram is known for minimizing production related cost, incorporation automation into its operations and pushing for improved supply occasioned by increase crop yield. OrganiGram’s cannabis-related biotechnology is at an advanced stage. In partnership with Hyasynth, OrganiGram intends to produce cannabinoids from cannabis-related plants. Consequently, a dip in production cost and processing durations are some of the expected gains of the proposed technology.

Looking ahead

Despite the favorable swings recorded by the Alternative Harvest ETF for the period (January to March 2019) under review, a considerable doubt still resides for these three stocks portfolios. Nevertheless, if the days ahead are still favorable, then predictions are that HEXO, Organic Green Dutchman, and OrganiGram, will retain their position as the market leader.
