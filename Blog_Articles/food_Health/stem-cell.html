How Stem Cells Can be Used to Fix Golf Elbow Pains

Golfers Elbow Pains are increasingly becoming significant and attracting attention in the medical parlance. Mostly common among sports men and women and caused by much stressed done to the muscles of the forearm. Consequently, it ensues pain to the sufferer. What exactly is a golfer elbow pain? Moreover, can stem cells be used to improve the situation? This article will provide answers to the above questions. However, before then, it is essential that you understand what stem cells mean and its importance.

Stem cells; Meaning and description

The cells in the human body have a specific function. However, there are those termed as stem cells. Stem cells do not yet have a particular purpose and as such, they can assume almost any cell that is required. In other words, stem cells are uniquely established cells which possess the capability to from specialized cell types in the human body. Under favorable conditions, whether in the body or laboratory, stem cells split to produce many other cells known as daughter cells. The daughter cells grow to replace specialized cells that are either lost or damaged in the body or form any other cell type that is in the human body.

Why are stem cells important?

Stem cells are increasingly attracting attention by Researchers and Doctors who now believe that stem cells assist in the following ways- Frist, they enhance the understanding of how diseases occur. Stems cells grow into cells in nerves, bones, heart muscles, tissues, and organ. Doctors monitor their evolution to have a better understanding of how disease conditions evolve. Second, they produce new and healthy cells to take place or restore diseased cells- Otherwise termed as regenerative medicine, stem cells can grow to function as specialized cell type; one that restores or repair damaged tissues in the human body. People suffering from diseases including spinal cord injuries, Parkinson’s disease, type 1 diabetes, heart disease, burns, stroke, osteoarthritis, and cancers can be managed with stem cell. They grow to form a new set of tissues for regenerative medicine and for transplanting. More so, they are used to examine new drugs and their effectiveness - Stem cells are also used to test the efficacy of a drug on specific cells and tissues of the body before the consumption of such medicines by people — for example; the generation of the nerve cells to examine the effectiveness of nerve disease.

What are the sources of Stem cells?

Embryonic stem cells: The embryonic stem cells are gotten from 3 to 5 years old embryos known as a blastocyst. The blastocyst has about 150 cells and is pluripotent i.e. capable of giving rise to several other types of cell in the body. This versatility characteristic allows the embryonic stem cells to be used for regenerate and repair damaged tissues.

Adult stem cells: They are present in small quantity in the adult tissues, including fat and the bone marrow. Previously, researchers suggested that unlike the embryonic stem cells, the adult stem cells have limited capability to generate the body severally. New trends now indicate the ability for the adult stem cells to be differentiated into several other cell types. Example; the heart muscle or bone cells can be created from the bone marrow stem cells.

Other stem cells include; induced pluripotent stem cells, which are stem cells generated by genetically reprogramming the adult cells. Another is the perinatal stem cells. The Perinatal stem cells are those stem cells that are present in amniotic fluid as well as umbilical cord blood. They can also change into specialized cells.
