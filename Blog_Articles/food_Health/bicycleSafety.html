Bicycle Safety

Bicycling is a typical means of transportation. In recent times, ownership increase with various uses including recreation, sport, and exercise.

With an estimated 100 million cycles, bicycle acquisition has attained its all-time surge. The growing demand for bi cycles means increased risk of injuries. As opined by the national statistics, about 1.8 billion biking occur annually. 494,000 crisis including abrasions, cuts, internal wounds, fractured bone, and other trauma occur while about 900 cyclists die yearly, 2000 others gain admittance into health facilities. Irrespective of the need, enormous risk follows biking thus the need to be particular about biking safety. So, let's consider what we can do to avoid biking related situations and death.

1. Safe Riding Tips

a. Use a safety helmet- Bicycle riding commences with wearing an helmet. The helmet protects your brains in the event of a crash and secures your life. However, for ultimate security, it is advisable that you acquire one that fits adequately. Wear it correctly and if need be, request assistance from someone to assist adjust the strap. Customize your helmet with a reflective sticker to make you visible to other motorists. Helmet arrives in various sizes and colors. So, stop by on Foota.co and make your choice from one of the several helmets.

b. Adjust Your Bicycle and check your equipment- Adjust the height of the seat to permit a modest bend at the knee whenever the leg is flexed. Similarly, position the handlebar at the same elevation as the seat. Sufficiently inflate the tires and examine your brakes before a ride.

c. See and get seen too- Irrespective the time of the day, - evening, morning or daytime, make sure you are visible. Colored clothing is not associated with visibility. Instead of neon, fluorescent, and other bright colors will do. Put on reflective marks. That a driver is visible to you may not mean you are visible to the driver. So be cautious.

d. Control Your Bicycle and avoid hazards- Do not bike without holding the handlebars. Carry objects in the backpack. Watch out for potential road dangers â€“ broken glass, stones, potholes, cars, human and animal traffic. They could cause a crisis. If in a group, alert others of the impending dangers.

e. Night riding is risky- It is dangerous to embark on night adventures. Visibility is difficult during this period. Should you go for a night ride, then appear in ways you are visible to others. The fore and rear of you bicycles should have a reflector- red and white light to the back and front respectively as enshrined by law.

2. Obey the rules of the Roads

In many towns and cities, bicycles are regarded as vehicles. So, just like other road users (motorists), cyclists possess similar rights and obligation to obey and maintain road rules when riding. A few of these rules are scheduled below-

a. Ride a bicycle with your two hands on the handlebars

b. While biking on the road, follow the same direction as traffic.

c. Observes road signs, obey traffic signs and m omentarily stop at red lights

d. If the road has a bike route, then follow suit

e. If you carry a passenger, he/she must wear a helmet

f. Move along the road in a line. Avoid the in and out of vehicles movement g. Be alert to your environment

h. If you need to turn, then signal other motorist coming behind, left or right

i. In a busy intersection, walk your bicycle across

j. Never go too close to a parked vehicle

k. If there is need to overtake, then do so to your left

l. Do not use a phone while riding. Put it away

m. Use the bell or horn where necessary

n. Make yourself visible and guide against drivers blind spot

o. If you must ride, then do not drink

p. Acquire a small tool kit
