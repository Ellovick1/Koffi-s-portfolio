How to Get Pregnant

There is no gainsaying that partners must attain good health to achieve pregnancy. However, good health is a necessary condition but not sufficient enough to guarantee pregnancy. In fact, couples at their fertility prime even without fertility challenges stand only a 24% likelihood of conceiving at the cycle. This implies that irrespective how excellent your health status, a high probability of difficulty to conceive is imminent. Attaining pregnancy is back-breaking to some people and effortless to others. Pregnancy may take up to a year for most couples and may persist even further in others. Therefore, it is essential, to understand that your influence over the length of time to conception is limited. However, be it as it may, there are a few actions you can undertake to improve your odds of conception.

1. Get Your Body Ready for Pregnancy
  • Check-in for a Prenatal investigation - Notwithstanding your fertility status, it is pertinent you opt-in for a prenatal examination since subsisting health-related issues becomes more severe at conception. Ideally, doctors perform inspections on the pelvis and make other health-related inferences including but not be limited to endometriosis, diabetes, Polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) and Thyroid disease. PCOS and endometriosis tend to hinder conception; thus the need for parental examination.
  • Maintain a healthy weight - Medically, underweighting or overweighting are not a healthy body condition. They are a pre-cursor to conception difficulty and may predispose the potential mother to pregnancy-related issues including stoppage of menstruation. Prior- to pregnancy, let your doctor work you through what an ideal weight range should be for you.
  • Consume prenatal vitamins - The embryo under formation requires nutrient. Therefore, Ingesting vitamins prior to conception reduces the risk of specific pregnancy-related issues. For instance, folic acid rich supplements lower the odds of spina bifida and other inadequacies including the neural tube. Allow your doctor advice and guide you on which supplements suits your body
  • Adhere to Your Diet Schedule - An appropriate diet schedule can incredibly improve your fertility status as well as your chances to fecundate. An ideal diet schedule is composed of fruits, vegetables, grains, and lean protein. Support your partner to avoid caffeine, sugar, fat and alcohol glut but rather consume vitamin-rich (E and C) diet such as vegetables to boosts sperm. More so, selenium improves men's fertility. So, about 55mcg of selenium per day is particularly ideal for the men.
  • Snap out of certain habit - Certain habits are not just good enough for partners actively seeking conception. These include smoking - (first and second hand), alcohol drinking, and caffeine intake. They predispose partners to a lower odds of conceiving. Caffeine intake comprising those from coffee tea, drinks, food, and chocolates should be restricted to about 200mg/day -2 cups or less of caffeinated beverages. Alcohol consumption and smoking reduce fertility in women, while sperm count and quality are negatively affected in men.
  • Visit a sex therapist or reproductive physician - Most times, either of the partners may not be interested in sex. Seek the advice of a licensed professional to assist surmount this challenge.
