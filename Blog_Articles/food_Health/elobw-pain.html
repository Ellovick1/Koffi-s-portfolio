Golfers Elbow Pain; meaning and description

The Golf Elbow which is otherwise known as epicondylitis is a medical condition which occurs when the inner side of the elbow is inflamed. This inflammation arises when the muscles of the forearm usually the pronator and flexor muscles become attached to the medial elbow.

What causes Golf Elbow Pains?

The golf elbow pains are caused by any activity which continuously stresses the muscles. These activities involve actions such as; gripping, flexing, swinging, backswing. It is not restricted to only the Golfers; it can arise from activities requiring the overuse of the flexors, including poor posture while working on the computer and from other manual labors. The degree of inflammation may differ from person to person. In the early stage, the pains are mild but become worst in the later stage.

What are the Symptoms of the Golfer’s Elbow?

The Symptoms of the golf elbow pains include the following

  • Numbness or tingling sensation radiating into the fingers
  • Inability to bend the elbow
  • Radiating pain into the hand
  • Deformed elbow
  • Stiffness in the elbow after playing golf
  • Weakness in the hands and wrists
  • Warm and inflamed elbow with fever
