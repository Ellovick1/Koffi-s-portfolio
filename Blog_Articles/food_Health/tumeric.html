Say Goodbye to Inflammation with Turmeric Supplements

Inflammation is the biological response of the body to pathogens and other harmful chemicals. It is often characterized by redness on some parts of the body, heat or swelling at the affected spots. The redness of the affected area is often the result of increased blood flow to the affected region more than it would typically have transmitted to the area. In other words, when inflammation occurs, white blood cells are released into the blood especially the affected tissues to protect the body from foreign substances. These blood cells fight against the incursion of unwanted chemicals into the body. It is this release of chemicals (white blood cells) in the body that inadvertently increases the blood flow to the area of injury or infection, and may result in redness and warmth.

Seemingly, the average human has likely been affected by inflammation at some points in their lives. After all, it is some defense mechanism against toxins and one way or the other we have all been exposed to harmful substances knowingly or unknowingly. Turmeric contains the active ingredient curcumin, which is known as an antioxidant with potent anti-inflammatory properties that can work effectively for health conditions such as arthritis, muscle sprains, and other injuries. About 500 milligrams of curcuminoids a day is an excellent wellness dose for keeping inflammation away and promoting gut health. In other words, the intake of turmeric not only helps reduce inflammation, but it also helps to keep the doctors away generally.

Symptoms of Inflammation

  • Pain: The inflamed area is likely to be painful to the individual especially for the first few minutes of the inflammation most especially, on touching.
  • Swelling: There is a buildup of fluid in the affected area which is as a result of increased blood flow into the region, i.e. the white blood cells. This could make the area increasingly sensitive to touch and could lead to swelling if the place is scratched.
  • Heat: Based upon the increasing presence of blood to the affected area, this makes it feel warm and more sensitive to the touch.
  • Redness: There is an increased presence of blood in the area, and this will reflect in the affected area.
