Presidential Debate- Buhari’s absent; Artiku walks away – My take


As the 2019 Presidential election draws near, the tension in the political space is heightened while expectations from the masses continue to surge. Seemingly, in other climes, to lie to bay the concern of the electorates, a debate is often the last resort. In our terrain, the reverse is the case. It is fascinating that an incumbent circumvents a presidential debate and even more worrisome that a chief contender jettisons it as well. However, with the melodramatic approach of these two, many have reasons to believe that Nigeria is set to enter into another 4years of Economic dip.


In a statement released by the opposition, Atiku pulled out of the debate because of President Buhari’s absence which he described as a slight on democracy and other candidates present for the debate. Buhari on the other hand through his Campaign spokesman Festus Keyamo mentioned that the arrangement did not suit hi (Buhari) and acknowledged that he was busy with official programs which clashed with the debate.

In the light of this event, the Nigerian populace is left with the option of wearing their emotions on their sleeves. The absence of PMB and Atiku is merely disrespectful and disgraceful. Why would a man at the helm of affairs circumvent the crucial time he is expected to challenge, be challenged and defend his numerous policies. Why should an erstwhile vice president and leader jettison the opportunity to pitch his plans for the Nation? Artiku claims a man’s head cannot be shaved in his absence, but seems to forget so soon that he who comes to the court of equity must do so with clean hands.


Nigeria falls below her potential. She has always been ruled and not led. So, she is in dire need to be rescued. We are disrespected, yet we don’t know that we are being disrespected. This generational disdain is the red line between doddering relics of the military-political combo and the quantum possibilities of a future Nigeria. Though I was never surprised at the show of shame by the two, in my opinion, both have confirmed their exit from the race. For the Nigerian people, this is an ample opportunity to make a clear judgment and salvage the Nigerian State. Remember, equity only aids the vigilant, not the indolent and does not require an idle gesture. Take action, vote wisely, vote for the future.
