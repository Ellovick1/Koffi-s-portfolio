Window and Sliding Glass Door Locks

The aesthetic attributes of glass doors and windows to building remains an aspect of a building that will always be admired. Despite their alluring effect on your homes, the truth about the sliding glass door and window is the fact that they arenâ€™t meant for every part of your home.

However, if you desire to fix them in some position against all the odds, you should know that; depending on their location in the building, they can either pose a significant challenge to homeowners or become a path through which burglars and criminal gain access into your building. Such an entrance, without surprise, usually leaves dent of theft and other forms of dangers to lives and properties.

What are my options? You may ask. It is simple! Equipping your window and sliding glass doors with secondary lock devices is an easy way to keep intruders away from your home. Read on to know what type of lock you can acquire on your sliding glass door and window to enhance the security of your home.

Security Bar

The security bar is a type of lock that fits perfectly for glass door and window. Security bars function by crossing a bar down to the bottom of the sliding track of the sliding door and window to restrict their movement. Also, to resist forced entry into buildings, security bars are built and fortified with strong metals.

Cylinder Lock

The cylinder lock is known to provide a world-class security system to glass door and window. Many people consider the cylinder lock safer and better since it prevents access to your property except by using its key. Cylinder locks can also be used together with a lever lock to enhance security. While using cylinder lock with lever lock, the door is locked unto a stationary bar right over the door frame giving it a rigid feel.

Clasp Lock

The clasp lock work with a small lever attached to the door to enhance the clasping mechanism. From the inside shaft of the door, the clasp raises to hook up to a small bar located in the interior part of the door or window. Turning down the lever of the door when not locked is the only way to open a clasp sliding glass door and window.


Mortise Lock

Another top-security lock type used for sliding glass door and windows is the mortise lock. The mortise lock arrives in different variations such as the lever or knob handle to open the door. Many people opt for the mortise lock since it is one of the bolts that is fitted into the door frame, hence making it difficult to break off or destroyed.
