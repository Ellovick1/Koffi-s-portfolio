Push and Pull Factors Influencing Inbound Tourists' Destination Choice: The Case of UAE

Abstract

This study done in five chapters reports the findings from a survey titled; Push and Pull Factors Influencing Inbound Tourists' Destination Choice for UAE. The study surveyed 150 inbound tourists to the UAE. It sought to identify the motives behind touristâ€™s decision to travel to the UAE, explore policies and strategies undertaken by the UAE government to boost tourism and identify unique features in the UAE that make it a popular tourist destination among other variables. Using the descriptive and inferential statistical analysis, the report is as follows.

Ordered from highest to least, the push motives influencing inbound tourism to UAE are; Association and social circle (4.28); eco-enhancement (4.21); escape (4.18); relation building (4.144) and sight-seeing and relaxation (4.143). The sixth is fulfilling prestige and self-esteem (4.142) while the least push factor is fulfilling spiritual needs (3.92). Similarly, the ranking pull factors for UAE tourism is follows; Events and varieties (4.27); second is historical antecedent (4.24); Easy access and affordability (4.14) is the third; cultural antecedent (4.03) is fourth. While infrastructure (3.91) is fifth, natural resources and attraction (3.86) is sixth. The result of the components is consistent with previous research on the push and pull factors of tourism.

The Pro-Tourism Policies, Single Government Tourism Authority at the Emirate, Tourism sustainability policy, Tourism Infrastructure Policy, Promoting, Marketing, Events, and Festivals Policy; State- Anchored Tourism Development Policy and Develop Tourism Products Types Policy are some of the strategies by government to develop the sector. The unique features in the UAE that make it a popular tourist destination include; famous events and landmarks, cost; 19(13%) travel to the UAE for their shopping needs; 14(9%) identified quality of service. While 14(9%) identified quality of service as a unique feature in UAE tourism, 8(5%) consider hospitality as the unique feature in UAE.

The empirical outcomes of this study confirms that inbound tourism to the UAE is closely associated with both push and pull factors, therefore government policies on inbound tourism should be a rich blend of the pull and push factor as represented in this study. With the ease in accessing UAE, marketing strategy would include the provision of service mostly at the strategic entry points including; the airports and use of every social media handles as well as the internet as sources of information. Other useful policy options are contained in the report.
