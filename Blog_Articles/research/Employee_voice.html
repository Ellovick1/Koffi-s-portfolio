Employee Voice and Silence

Introduction

Performance feedback, i.e. information about one’s performance, is widely believed to contribute to performance improvement. However, it has long been clear that people are reluctant to provide others with feedback; it is even more difficult for employees to gain certain resources through a formal human resource system in a timely fashion. Instead of passively waiting (silence) for feedback to be provided, employees may actively seek feedback (voice) to adapt and develop. Feedback seeking can generate a wide range of benefits for employees, from the question of “how am I doing” to survive and fit in to the question of “am I on the right track to achieve the future goals” to develop and grow. However, when this form of feedback is insufficient, employees actively seek informal types of feedback through direct inquiry, indirect inquiry, and observing their environment. It is possible that individuals may choose certain feedback-seeking strategies over others, depending on perceptions of their supervisor’s relationship with them and their own communication traits. In the light of this, the Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) and other measures becomes handy to elucidating information in this regards. Scholars have suggested that leaders in high LMX relationships tend to expect their followers to fulfill the reciprocity obligations for long-term personal development and collective interests instead of short-term personal gratification. It makes sense that those leaders who are able to communicate competently with their subordinates will more likely develop high quality relationships and interpersonal communication competence. Apparently, the present study set out to examine Managers LMX. It is conducted by serving 5 questionnaires to 5 Managers of Greek enterprises. The questionnaires are structured to captures questions on selected variables such as “Leader-Member Exchange” (LMX), “Feedback Seeking Behavior” (FSB) and “Leader Identification” (LI). Completed questionnaires were collected and analyze using SPSS and the results were tested on selected demographic (control) variable of age, working experience and educational level.
