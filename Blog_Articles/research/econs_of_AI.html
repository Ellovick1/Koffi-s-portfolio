The Economics of Artificial Intelligence: Implications for the Future of Work

Abstract

The present wave of machine and technological variations consequence on the emergence of artificial intelligence (AI) has produced widespread concerns over job losses. Fears are further exacerbated over the anticipated and potential inequality gap. For this reason, this study sought to examine the economics of artificial intelligence and its implications for the future of work. It followed the quantitative and qualitative based approaches to analyze the objectives. Current evidence suggest that it is not possible to conclude whether the emergence of AI-based applications will lead to widespread job losses or increase inequality. The effects of AI are dependent on its area of usage, innovations and technology. Further, the study confirmed a positive relationship between application of Artificial intelligence with job improvement, productivity, increase revenue and job losses. The need to adequately support the adjustments in the labor market for a proper transition to jobs which offers comparative advantage to employees, reinforcing guarantees and assurances of equal playing field to foster competitiveness and guide against market dominance by some firms, the use of fiscal measures such as tax and social protection to forestall the effect of current transformation in the labor market, and finally, fostering international cooperation as well as social discuss among countries are some policy propositions.

Keywords: Artificial intelligence, inequality, fiscal measures, technological unemployment
