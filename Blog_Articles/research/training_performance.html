Impact of Training on the Performance and Productivity of IT firms

1.0 Objective and Research Question

Firms including those of the Information Technology embarks on staff training to enhance their productivity. The present study seeks to examine the influence of such training on organizational performance. The objectives put forward included; to understand the socio-demographics of the employees, to evaluate the impacts of training on the employees, understand the nexus which exist between organizational productivity and employee training, verify if training outcome in males significantly differ from the outcome in female staff and examine the effect which periodic training has on the productivity of Information Technology firms. The population used for the study was about 70 employees from the IT industry, sampled from LinkedIn professional platform. Primary source of data; mainly the questionnaire was used to gather responses. Statistical Package for Social Sciences (SPSS) was used to analyze the gathered data. The descriptive and inferential statistics were used to analyze the objectives. Preliminary investigation shows that 66% of the respondents are men while about 34% are female. The cohorts are mainly from private firms (64%). Majority (60%) possess bachelorâ€™s degree and have over 11-15 years of experience. Using the correlation coefficient, regression, independent and paired sample test, hypotheses were tested. The paired sample t test revealed that after training, there was a significant difference in the scores of firm productivity PRO1 (M = 19.97, SD = 1.88) and PRO2 (M = 21.77, SD = 1.94), t (-5.00) = 69, p < 0.05. There is no significant difference in the scores of male staff (M = 21.80, SD = 1.93) from those of the female staff (M = 21.70, SD = 1.98), t (1.95) = 68, p = 0.846 as indicated by the independent sample t test. The relationship between training organizational performance is positive (r= 0.52, n= 70, p < 0.05) while the regression technique shows a positive effect (b= 0.19, p < 5%) between staff training and IT firm productivity. Based on this, the study advocates the institutionalization of training programmes for all employees in the various departments of IT firms to enable them become more equipped and ready to efficiently discharge their duties qualitatively and in record time.

Keywords: Staff Training, Productivity, Information Technology, Organizational Performance
