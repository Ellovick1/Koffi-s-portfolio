The Effect of Economic Growth On Carbon Emission; A Case of the United Kingdom

1.0 Introduction

According to World Bank (2009), CO2emissions mainly from energy consumption is closely related to economic growth. The industrialized economies have been accused of most anthropogenic greenhouse gases emitted. In 2008, two thirds of the world emissions were said to be caused by about 10 of these countries. With threat of global warming on the world ecosystem, the Climate Change Framework Convention and Kyoto Protocol; a political and geographical effort aimed at reducing climate change was initiated (Halıcıoğlu, 2009). Signing this framework has caused heated discussions on the relationship between economic growth and CO2emissions.

However, numerous studies have examined the nexus between CO2 emission and economic growth (see, HamitHaggar, 2012, Du et al., 2012, Iwata et al., 2010, Tiwari et al., 2013, Shahbaz et al., 2013, Pao et al., 2011, Esteve and Tamarit, 2012, Yavuz, 2014, Onafowora and Owoye, 2014 Jumbe, 2004; Al-Iriani, 2006; Ang, 2007; Halicioglu, 2007, Halicioglu, 2009; Soytas et al., 2007; Sheinbaum-Pardo et al., 2012; Lean and Smith, 2009; Chang et al., 2009; Apergis and Payne, 2009, 2010; Bartleet and Gounder, 2010; Menyah and Rufael, 2010; Ozturk and Acaravci, 2010; Niu et al., 2011; Arouri et al., 2012).

The empirical outcomes of many of these studies differ and in most cases, conflicting. This paper gives empirical evidence for the UK economy.
