The Impact of Building Information Modelling (BIM) System On Quantity Surveying Roles in the Construction Industry

Abstract

Building Information Modelling (BIM) improves construction management. The increasing demand for quality, cost minimization, and environmentally sustainable operations, has led to the adoption of BIM by major players including Quantity Surveyors. The current study attempt to understand the impact of BIM in construction, the roles quantity surveyors play in the construction industry, the changes observed in construction consequent upon the introduction of BIM and the impact of BIM on Quantity surveying. A quantitative research method was adopted. By way of a questionnaire survey, primary data was collected from 50 construction industry practitioners and analyzed using descriptive statistic method. Result show that many of the respondents (32%) works for private firms with majority practicing quantity surveying. BIM adoption is low among earlier industry practitioners when compared to new recruits. The most widely use BIM software is Autodek Naviswork. Fewer change orders, improved communications, improved cost estimating, reduced overall project cost, enhanced work condition, greater productivity due to easy retrieval of information and generation of accurate and consistent 2D drawing at any stage are highly rated benefits of BIM. Cost of training existing staffs, undefined responsibilities for the data content clients driven limitations, unawareness of BIM benefits, cost of employing additional staff, the need for well-defined commercial business process model and the lack of clear boundary of responsibilities between parties are some barriers to BIM. Awareness creation and support by stakeholders, training and restraining of staff to boost expatriates are some ways targeted at increasing BIM adoption level

Key words: Building Information Modelling, Quantity Surveying, Construction management
