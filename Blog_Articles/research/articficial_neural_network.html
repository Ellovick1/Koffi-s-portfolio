Artificial Neural Networks and Pathologists Recognize Basal Cell Carcinomas Based on different Histological patterns

Abstract

Histopathological images are captured to locate, examine, and classify diseases like cancer. They provide a detailed view of diseases and their tissue statuses. These images are an essential resource with which to define biological compositions or analyze cell and tissue structures. However, artificial intelligence in recent times has given advances in the field of deep learning which have helped researchers with medical image algorithms analysis. Deep learning–based diagnostic methods, for analyzing histological patterns of basal cell carcinomas (BCCs) are well suited for automated high-resolution microscopy images by neural networks. This study utilized accurately an intuitively interpretable artificial neural network (ANN) in analysis of histological patterns of basal cell carcinomas (BCCs) in histological whole slide images versus that of pathologists. Also, the detection of histological whole slide images of basal cell carcinomas (BCCs). A trained ANN on whole slide images of BCCs tumor regions identification (n=819). The relevant regions for diagnosis by the ANN compared to regions of interest for pathologists, detected by eye-tracking techniques. The ANN identified accurately tumor regions of BCC on images of histologic slides (AUC: 0.991, 95% CI: 0.993-0.996; sensitivity: 0.967, 95% CI: 0.950-0.977; specificity: 0.913, 95% CI: 0.857-0.961). A weight matrix calculated by ANN implicitly indicated the regions of a histological image that are important for the prediction of the network. Amazingly, machine learning algorithms compared to pathologists’ eye tracking results, rely on significantly different patterns of recognition for tumor identification (p< 10-4). In conclusion, based on the accessible BCC microscopic ocular images, the state-of-art machine learning techniques can efficiently and interpretably analyze BCC histopathological images. Furthermore, the diagnostic precision in digital pathology can be enhanced by neural networks and algorithms which in turn uncover unused classification patterns.

Introduction

Artificial intelligence (AI) although widely accepted in the medical field has really grown in recent times and promises to take care of patients at the next level with the aim of expanding and improving medicine through the use of individual patients. The power of AI in medicine is limitless and can serve as a great blessing to improve health delivery in clinical practice. Digital pathology can be interpreted as a clinical definition and data management from digitalized slides, with the aim of improving the safety, quality and accuracy of disease diagnosis (Griffin et al., 2017). The scope of digital pathology can be felt in combination with machine learning that is far beyond modern possibilities (Parwani, 2019). Digital histopathological slides are easy to share, collaborate, produce instructional sets and include minimal preparation time.
