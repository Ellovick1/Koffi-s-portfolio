Impact of Working Capital Management Policy of the Profitability of Food Industry in Saudi Arabia

Abstract

This study investigated the impact of Working Capital Management Policies (WCMP) on the profitability of food industry in Saudi Arabia using a panel data set for the period 2007 to 2016. Preliminary investigations showed no unit root property. The Ordinary Least Squares regression technique was implored on the explanatory variables; average collection period (ACP), a proxy for the collection policy; inventory conversion period (ITD) used as a proxy for the inventory policy; average payment period (APP) used as a proxy for the payment policy; cash conversion cycle (CCC) used as a comprehensive measure for working capital management; and the current ratio (CAR). The Hausman test supported estimates from fixed effects having refuted the null hypothesis (random effect is appropriate). Cash conversion policy and average payment period significantly explained the variations witnessed in gross operating profit of food and nutrition industry in Saudi Arabia. In view of this, ensuring shorter cash conversion period by investing more in current asset and forging strong alliance between food firms and its suppliers to strategically improve production lines and strengthen credit records of firms are policy options.

Keywords: Unit root, Ordinary Least Squares, Hausman test, Working capital management
